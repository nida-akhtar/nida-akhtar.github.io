01 / Research Interests

Questions I'd love to spend years on.

My current research interests, broadly. These have shifted over time and will likely continue to.

Computer Vision

Visual understanding

A model that can identify 1,000 dog breeds will sometimes mistake a school bus for an ostrich. That's the part I find interesting.

Applied ML

ML pipelines in production

I'm interested in the work of moving machine learning from research notebooks into reliable production systems: data validation, model drift, monitoring, and the engineering practices that make deployed ML trustworthy over time.

Multimodal Models

Text + vision + everything

Models that combine modalities are some of the most exciting works happening right now. How do they fuse signals? Where do they fail? How do we evaluate them honestly?

Health × ML

AI in Healthcare

I'm becoming more and more interested in how machine learning can be applied to healthcare. Medical imaging, diagnostics, and clinical tools are areas where good models can genuinely help people, but they're also areas where getting things wrong has real consequences. I think that tension is what makes the space worth taking seriously.

Topics I've worked on and continue to explore

Topic
Working with scarce data
How to train good models when high-quality data is scarce, and what techniques like synthetic data generation, augmentation, and self-supervised learning can offer. This question matters most in fields where labelled data is expensive or simply doesn't exist yet.
Topic
Efficient inference & quantization
Making models small without making them dumb. Practical compression for edge and CPU.
Topic
Evaluation & failure modes
A model that gets 95% on a benchmark can still be uselessly wrong in deployment. How do we measure what matters?
