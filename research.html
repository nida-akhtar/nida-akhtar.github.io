01 / Research Interests

Questions I'd love to spend years on.

A snapshot of the topics that pull my attention right now. This list shifts as I read, build, and stumble into new things — which is half the fun.

Computer Vision

Visual understanding

From classical detection to vision-language models — I'm drawn to how machines actually see versus how we think they see. Especially interested in the gap between benchmark performance and real-world robustness.

Applied ML

ML pipelines in production

The craft of turning a notebook into a system that doesn't fall over at 3am. Data validation, drift, monitoring, and the boring infrastructure work that makes ML actually useful.

Multimodal Models

Text + vision + everything

Models that combine modalities are some of the most exciting work happening right now. How do they fuse signals? Where do they fail? How do we evaluate them honestly?

Backend × ML

Systems for ML

Inference servers, vector databases, retrieval pipelines, low-latency model serving — the engineering side of intelligence. This is where my backend roots meet my ML curiosity.

A note on focus

I tell myself I'll pick one area and go deep — and I do, eventually. But I also believe lateral curiosity is its own kind of depth. The best ideas I've had came from connecting two things that didn't seem related at first.
