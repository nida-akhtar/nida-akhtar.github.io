04 / Off the keyboard

Life beyond the terminal.

The things I do when I'm not writing code or training models. They keep me grounded, and I think they make me a better engineer too.

Calligraphy

This is the hobby I'm most serious about. There's something about putting pen to paper, watching ink shape itself into a letter, that nothing on a screen can replicate. It's slow, it's quiet, and it's the opposite of debugging — which is exactly why I love it.

I share my work on Instagram at @calligraphy_on_page if you'd like to take a look.

Cooking

I love cooking. There's something genuinely satisfying about following a process, getting the timing right, and ending up with something the people you love can sit down and enjoy. It's a different kind of problem-solving, and one of my favorite ways to unwind.

Cats

I'm firmly in the cat-person camp. I find their independence funny, their judgement of humans accurate, and their company strangely calming.

Tea, and good conversation

A cup of tea is non-negotiable in my day. And honestly, the best parts of my week tend to involve tea, family, and the kind of long, unhurried conversations that you only have with people you really know. I'm quiet around new people, but with the right company, I can talk for hours.

Sports & community

I enjoy badminton and cricket — both for the games themselves and for the people you end up playing with. I also genuinely enjoy organizing events: the planning, the logistics, the small satisfaction of seeing something you put together actually come to life.
